Bobby was fuming. He didn't know what he deserved to get it, but get it he did. It was very surprising for him; one minute he was playing around on the floor, the next minute he was getting repremanded by a teacher, face as red as an apple. Through the stern looks and exploding voices, he interpreted essentially nothing, and was shoved out of the classroom. He stood there, still in a phase of disbelief, unable to comprehend the series of events that just took place.

The door opened, and another kid stepped out. Her posture was crooked, bent over. Half her face was red, as if only one of her eyes had agreed on crying.

"Hi," she said in a shaky little voice.

"Hi," he replied, with as much warmth as he could muster.

She took two steps backwards, and headed to the washroom.

"Wait!" he cried.

But she never looked back.
